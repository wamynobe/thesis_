\chapter*{Abstract}

Mandarin Chinese is spoken by approximately 1.14 billion people worldwide, with an estimated 199 million second-language learners~\cite{icls2026}. Due to its increasing importance in global communication, business, and technology, Mandarin has become a key target language for many learners.
A major challenge in learning Mandarin lies in its tonal nature, where pitch patterns at the syllable level are phonemic and directly affect word meaning. For learners whose native languages are non-tonal, such as Polish, acquiring correct tonal production is particularly difficult.
Recent advances in computer-assisted pronunciation training (CAPT) systems have leveraged machine learning techniques to provide automated feedback on pronunciation, especially in tone recognition. With the rapid development of deep learning, tone classification methods have evolved from convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to self-supervised learning (SSL) models, significantly improving performance.
This study investigates and compares multiple tone classification approaches, including CNN-based and SSL-based models, for assessing Mandarin pronunciation of Polish learners. The goal is to identify the most effective modeling strategy for accurate tone classification aligned with expert evaluation.
