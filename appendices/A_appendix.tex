\section{Self-Supervised Speech Models}
\label{sec:app:ssl_hparams}

Table~\ref{tab:app:ssl_common_hparams} gives the common learner fine-tuning
settings used by the SSL systems unless otherwise stated. All SSL experiments
operate on raw 16\,kHz waveforms and use the same speaker-disjoint learner
split. The backbone choices follow Wav2Vec~2.0 and HuBERT
architectures~\cite{baevski2020,hsu2021}.

The unfreezing point and the total update budget were refined by the Optuna
sensitivity study in Appendix~\ref{sec:app:sensitivity}; the post-Optuna
schedule applies to Setups 3, 5 and 6. Setups 2 and 4 were trained earlier
with the pre-Optuna schedule (\texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}\,=\,5\,000,
\texttt{TOTAL\_UPDATES}\,=\,30\,000) and were not re-run after the Optuna
study. For Setup~4, this matches the within-search optimum confirmed by
Study~2 for the Mandarin Wav2Vec~2.0 backbone.

The frame-voting SSL setups use frame-level cross-entropy on MFA-masked learner
frames (adapted from Yuan et~al.~\cite{yuan2023}) and majority-vote utterance
prediction at inference. Learner development accuracy selects the frame-voting
learner checkpoints, while the HuBERT attention model is selected by learner
development macro-F1 and the AISHELL-1 intermediate checkpoints are selected by
native-speech frame accuracy.

The AISHELL-1 intermediate stage used 33\,986 training utterances and 693
development utterances after filtering. Its frame-label inventories
(Table~\ref{tab:design:aishell_label_streams}) were:
\texttt{label1} with \(T_1\)--\(T_4\) plus ignored neutral-tone and silence
frames, and \texttt{label2} and \texttt{label3} with one additional ignored
non-tone class plus ignored neutral-tone and silence frames. The fifth learner
category \(T_{\mathrm{NS}}\) is not supervised on AISHELL-1 and is randomly
re-initialised before learner fine-tuning.

\section{Hyperparameter Sensitivity}
\label{sec:app:sensitivity}

Four schedule hyperparameters in Table~\ref{tab:app:ssl_common_hparams} are
not specified by Yuan et~al.~\cite{yuan2023}: the frozen-phase learning rate
\texttt{LR\_HEAD}, the unfreezing point \texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}, the linear
warm-up length \texttt{WARMUP\_UPDATES}, and the total update budget
\texttt{TOTAL\_UPDATES}. These four values were verified through three
Optuna studies~\cite{akiba2019optuna} on development-set performance. The
fixed values shared across all studies (Yuan's \texttt{LR\_FULL}, the
frame-level cross-entropy objective, and the pretrained checkpoint of each
study's target setup) are held constant so that the search isolates the
contribution of the schedule; Study~3 additionally fixes the AISHELL-1
label-stream choice to \texttt{label2}. Each study enqueues the pre-Optuna
baseline configuration as its first trial so that the baseline always has a
reference value in the resulting scatter plots.

\subsection{Study 1: Setup 5 - Chinese HuBERT-Large with frame-level CE}
\label{sec:app:sensitivity:hubert}

Setup~5 is the strongest configuration in Chapter~\ref{chapter:5_Experiments}
and is therefore taken as the primary sensitivity target. All four schedule
hyperparameters are searched. The study ran 15 Optuna trials with the
TPE sampler and a MedianPruner~\cite{akiba2019optuna} activated at update~7\,000.

Of 15 trials, 10 ran to completion and 5 were pruned. Two pruned trials
(numbers~7 and~9) combined an \texttt{LR\_HEAD}\,$\gtrsim 2{\times}10^{-4}$
with a late unfreezing point (\texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}\,=\,7\,000--10\,000) and
collapsed to near-chance development macro-F1 (0.37--0.39) by update~7\,000.
The other three pruned trials (numbers~10, 11, and~14) shared the best
schedule profile (\texttt{FREEZE}\,=\,2\,000, \texttt{WARMUP}\,=\,2\,000,
\texttt{TOTAL}\,=\,40\,000) and reached macro-F1 between 0.69 and 0.76 before
being stopped by the median pruner for not improving fast enough relative to
the running leader, rather than for collapsing. The top three completed trials
all share \texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}\,=\,2\,000, \texttt{WARMUP\_UPDATES}\,=\,2\,000,
and \texttt{TOTAL\_UPDATES}\,=\,40\,000, with \texttt{LR\_HEAD} spread over
nearly a decade ($2.5{\times}10^{-5}$ to $1.7{\times}10^{-4}$); macro-F1 is
therefore essentially insensitive to \texttt{LR\_HEAD} within a wide range.
The pre-Optuna baseline reaches 0.7255, which is approximately 3.9 percentage
points below the empirical optimum but in line with the other moderate-scoring
trials. The chosen post-Optuna schedule for Setups~3, 5, and 6 uses
\texttt{FREEZE}\,=\,2\,000 and \texttt{TOTAL}\,=\,40\,000 directly, but
retains \texttt{WARMUP}\,=\,1\,000 as a middle ground between the value
preferred here (2\,000) and the value preferred in Study~3 (0). Setup~2 was
trained before the Optuna study and uses the pre-Optuna schedule
(\texttt{FREEZE}\,=\,5\,000, \texttt{TOTAL}\,=\,30\,000); it was not re-run
after the study.

\subsection{Study 2: Setup 4 - Chinese Wav2Vec~2.0-Large}
\label{sec:app:sensitivity:chinese_w2v}

Setup~4 collapses on \(T_{\mathrm{NS}}\) in the main experiments
(Section~\ref{sec:exp:class_level}). This study tests whether the
collapse is a hyperparameter artefact or a property of the Mandarin-pretrained
representation. The search is deliberately narrow: \texttt{LR\_HEAD} and
\texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES} only, with \texttt{WARMUP} and \texttt{TOTAL} fixed
at the main-run values so that the two variables most likely to influence
class-balanced behaviour are isolated. The trial reports both dev macro-F1 and dev \(T_{\mathrm{NS}}\) recall.

Of 10 trials, only 3 ran to completion and 7 were pruned. Two of the pruned
trials (numbers~5 and~9) combined \texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}\,=\,15\,000 with a
moderate \texttt{LR\_HEAD} and stalled near 0.24 macro-F1; trial~7 used a
very high \texttt{LR\_HEAD}\,$\approx 1.8{\times}10^{-4}$ and was pruned at
macro-F1\,$\approx$\,0.48. The remaining four pruned trials (numbers~3, 4, 6,
and~8) used moderate hyperparameters but were stopped between update~7\,000
and~18\,000 at intermediate macro-F1 between 0.21 and 0.62, because they were
not improving fast enough relative to the leading completed trials.
The Mandarin-pretrained Wav2Vec backbone therefore has a narrower viable
hyperparameter region than HuBERT (Setup~5 pruned only 5/15 trials, and only
two of those at near-chance scores). Within this search space, no completed
trial moved dev \(T_{\mathrm{NS}}\) recall above 0.35, and the 25\,\% Setup~4
test \(T_{\mathrm{NS}}\) recall reported in Section~\ref{sec:exp:class_level}
is therefore not plausibly recovered by re-tuning \texttt{LR\_HEAD} and
\texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES} alone. The collapse is not resolved by these two
hyperparameters; whether it can be resolved by a wider sweep
(loss function, pooling, class weighting, total-update budget, calibration,
seed) is not tested here, so the Section~\ref{sec:exp:interpretation}
interpretation that attributes the residual gap to the Mandarin-pretrained
representation is consistent with this study but not proven by it. The
baseline schedule for Setup~4 is retained in the main runs because it is the
within-search optimum for that backbone.

\subsection{Study 3: AISHELL-1 Stage A}
\label{sec:app:sensitivity:aishell}

Yuan et~al.~\cite{yuan2023} tuned their Stage~A schedule on AISHELL-1 frame
labels derived from an HMM-GMM aligner; this thesis uses Montreal Forced
Aligner~\cite{mcauliffe2017mfa} instead. We therefore re-tune the Stage~A
schedule on the MFA-derived frame labels rather than re-using Yuan's
HMM-GMM-derived schedule unchanged. All four schedule hyperparameters are
searched on the \texttt{label2} stream (the stream with the highest AISHELL-1
development frame accuracy, which is the Stage~A checkpoint-selection
metric). The objective is the AISHELL-1 development frame accuracy over
\(T_1\)--\(T_4\), which matches the metric used to select the Stage~A

which justifies the documented deviation in

\subsection{Cross-study summary}
\label{sec:app:sensitivity:summary}

Three findings recur across the studies and motivate the schedule used in
the main experiments:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Earlier unfreeze is consistently better.}
  Studies~1 and~3 both find \texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}\,=\,2\,000 optimal in
  their search range; values $\geq$\,10\,000 (including Yuan et~al.'s
  \texttt{FREEZE\_UPDATES}\,=\,10\,000) are at best uncompetitive and at
  worst catastrophic. The exception is Setup~4, where Study~2 confirmed 5\,000
  within its search range.
  \item \textbf{A longer update budget helps.}
  Both Studies~1 and~3 prefer \texttt{TOTAL\_UPDATES}\,=\,40\,000 over the
  pre-Optuna value of 30\,000. The main learner runs adopt 40\,000.
  \item \textbf{\texttt{LR\_HEAD} sits on a plateau around $10^{-4}$.}
  Studies~1--3 each place their best \texttt{LR\_HEAD} between
  $9\times10^{-5}$ and $1.7\times10^{-4}$, and Study~1 shows that
  performance is essentially flat over almost a decade around that range.
  The chosen value $1\times10^{-4}$ remains in the safe plateau.
\end{enumerate}
